# IBD Emotion Taxonomy

Complete emotion profile data for all 76 emotion tokens.

## Overview

These profiles are designed to be ingested into the **Chromatic Emotion Architecture (CEA)** as the prose/curatorial layer complementing the OKLCH perceptual color ranges.

Each profile includes:
- **Definition** — Curatorial description of the emotion
- **Color Palette** — 6 hex colors (Primary 3 + Accent 3) with pairing notes
- **Textures** — Surface qualities associated with the emotion
- **Shapes** — Geometric and organic forms
- **Art Styles** — Historical and contemporary style references
- **Art Mediums** — Physical and digital medium recommendations
- **Suggested Placements** — Context-specific installation guidance
- **Complement & Contrast** — Relationship mapping to other emotions
- **Use Cases** — Specific application examples with descriptions
- **Tags** — Keyword associations

**Integration Note:** The hex color palettes here are reference palettes that should be mapped to OKLCH equivalents during CEA integration. The definitions, placements, and use cases are hand-authored curatorial content.

#### Serenity

**Slug:** `serenity`
**Keywords:** serene, inner peace, calm, placid

**Definition:**
A profound sense of undisturbed calm that transcends momentary relaxation. Serenity in art creates spaces where the viewer's internal noise quiets, inviting deep presence and emotional stillness.

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: `#B8D4E3` | `#E8F0F2` | `#6A9FB5`
- Accent: `#D4C5A9` | `#C2D8B9` | `#F5F0E8`
- *Pairing Notes:* Cool, muted tones dominate. Pair with warm neutrals to prevent coldness. Avoid high-saturation accents.

**Textures:** Smooth watercolor washes, Soft gradient transitions, Matte finishes, Linen or cotton canvas texture

**Shapes:**
- Geometric: Wide horizontal bands, Gentle arcs, Circles
- Organic: Rolling hills, Calm water surfaces, Cloud formations

**Art Styles:** Impressionism, Minimalism, Japanese Zen, Tonalism

**Art Mediums:** Watercolor, Soft pastel, Photography (long exposure), AI-generated atmospheric landscapes

**Suggested Placements:**
- Master Bedroom: Above the headboard to set a restful tone
- Meditation Room: Centered on the focal wall
- Spa Reception: Behind the check-in desk to immediately signal calm
- Therapist Office: In the client's line of sight for grounding

**Relationships:**
- Complements: Tranquility, Peace, Stillness, Clarity
- Contrasts: Energy, Vibrancy, Dynamism, Tension

**Use Cases:**
- *Luxury Spa:* Soft watercolor triptych in muted blues and warm ivory, creating a visual exhale as clients enter
- *Residential Bedroom:* Large-format minimalist horizon line in pale blue and linen tones, mounted frameless on gallery wrap

**Tags:** Peaceful, Still, Meditative, Quiet, Undisturbed
